you have every right to be upset or sad that a tool you use is shutdown -- i get that, i also hate change. this however **DOES NOT** give you a free pass to take out your frustrations on the developers of said tool (or similar ones). i don't care if the views of a developer don't match yours, it still doesn't give you the right to spew the hate you have been. _nobody_ deserves the level of hate that some of these developers have received.

**stop biting the hand that feeds you.**

take a step back from the computer. go outside, touch some grass perhaps. remember that there are **REAL** people behind these apps. people that have given up (potentially massive) chunks of their free time out of sheer goodwill to develop and create something for _you, the user_.

i haven't been in the plural community long (if you wanna know, go check the creation date on our PK system or something). the sheer amount of hate however that we've seen in such a relatively small period of time is absurd. we personally started to volunteer our time to PK in the first place because it was one of the most helpful tools to us in our journey, and we wanted to give back.

i... sincerely hope the community learns from this.  
_spoiler alert: if this keeps up, people are likely just gonna stop making apps for the plural community_

on the other hand as a quick note, if it wasn't clear enough, i do wanna acknowledge that it is just a very vocal minority that are causing the most problems. thank you to those who have actually been kind.

## re: alternatives to SP/Octo - my advice

are you concerned about privacy? use a physical journal and lock it up or use some sort of local encrypted note taking software. seriously. with any online tools you're putting your trust into a third party -- whether they earn your trust or not is fully up to you.

the other reason why i'm writing this post however:

{% callout type="warning_but_louder" %}
BE CAREFUL WITH NEW APPS
{% /callout %}

a lot of new apps are popping up in quite a short time. be wary of apps that are [vibecoded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vibe_coding) as they can have massive security risks (in addition to just... being... ew.) -- especially if they do any sort of online syncing.  
i'm not saying don't trust anyone. i'm saying use your common sense.  
look into apps before putting private system data in them.  
also -- and i can't believe i have to say this -- don't be an asshole and slander apps just because you don't like them for whatever reason.

i will list a few apps here that I personally have heard of and trust
but do your own research:

- [Ampersand](https://ampersand.moe)
- [Lighthouse](https://www.writelighthouse.com)
- [/plu/ral](https://plural.gg)
- [PluralKit](https://pluralkit.me)

(non plural specific)

- [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md)
